I'm Jok Akech Atem Mabior, a Master of Science in Engineering AI student at Carnegie Mellon University Africa in Kigali, Rwanda. Originally from South Sudan, I am driven by a deep commitment to leveraging artificial intelligence and machine learning for social impact across the African continent.

My research and engineering interests sit at the intersection of Natural Language Processing, Reinforcement Learning, and Computer Vision — with a focus on building systems that work equitably for underrepresented communities and low-resource languages in East Africa.

Before CMU Africa, I gained industry experience at technology companies in South Sudan and Uganda, where I built and deployed data-driven solutions in resource-constrained environments. I believe in responsible AI that centres the needs of African communities.
